## Design Context

### Users
AI engineering leads, startup founders, and technical decision-makers evaluating whether to hire or collaborate with Yen Wee. They arrive from LinkedIn, Medium articles, or referrals. They're scanning for credibility signals — production experience, technical depth, and professionalism — not browsing for entertainment. The job to be done: decide in under 30 seconds whether this person is worth a call.

### Brand Personality
**Competent. Professional. Thought Leader.**

Voice is direct and evidence-based — metrics over adjectives, shipped systems over promises. Tone is confident without being boastful; the work speaks. No playfulness, no quirky copy, no casual energy. This is someone who builds production systems at scale and writes about AI with authority.

Emotional goal: visitors should feel "this is premium quality" — the same reaction as opening a well-engineered product.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual Tone:** Minimal, precise, premium. Clean negative space. Every element earns its place.

**Color System:**
- Primary palette is achromatic (OKLCH grayscale) — this is intentional and core to the identity
- Accent: Muted violet (`rgba(100, 100, 255, 0.12)` in light / `rgba(200, 200, 255, 0.1)` in dark) — already present in SpotlightCard hover glow. This should be the formalized accent color for interactive states, subtle highlights, and motion effects
- The violet accent reads as intellectual and premium without being playful — it bridges the gap between "technical" and "refined"
- Avoid saturated colors, warm tones, or gradients that feel startup-y or consumer-app-ish

**Typography:**
- Geist Sans — the same font Vercel uses. Clean, modern, engineered feel
- Headings: font-light (thin weight creates sophistication)
- Labels/metadata: font-mono, tracking-wider, uppercase — creates systematic precision
- Body: regular weight, relaxed leading for readability

**Motion:**
- Purposeful, not decorative. Scroll-triggered fade-in-up reveals, staggered children, decrypted text effects
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` — a custom spring curve used consistently throughout
- Reduced intensity: gentle floats, subtle card lifts, no bouncing or aggressive animations
- Threads background: low-opacity generative art that adds depth without distraction

**Layout:**
- Max-width 6xl (1152px), generous horizontal padding (24-32px)
- Section-based vertical rhythm with scroll navigation
- Mobile-first responsive with clean breakpoints

**Theme:** Full dark/light mode support with smooth 300ms transitions. Dark mode is the primary experience (matches developer/technical audience expectation).

**References:**
- linear.app — precision, dark-mode-first, developer credibility
- vercel.com — Geist typography, premium developer feel, clean product presentation
- rauno.me — personal dev portfolio with tasteful motion
- paco.me — ultra-minimal, lets the work speak

**Anti-References:**
- Nothing playful, quirky, or illustration-heavy
- No bright gradients, emoji, or consumer-app aesthetics
- No Dribbble-style showcase layouts
- No corporate enterprise feel (no stock photos, no generic hero sections)

### Design Principles

1. **Substance over style.** Every visual choice should reinforce credibility. Decoration without purpose gets cut.

2. **Precision is the aesthetic.** Consistent spacing, systematic typography, deliberate color — the beauty comes from engineering, not ornamentation.

3. **Let the work breathe.** Generous whitespace, restrained density. Premium quality means not cramming. Every section earns its space.

4. **Motion with intent.** Animations reveal content and guide attention — they never perform for their own sake. If removing an animation loses nothing, remove it.

5. **Dark-mode native.** Design for dark first, adapt to light. The technical audience expects it, and the achromatic palette shines in it.
