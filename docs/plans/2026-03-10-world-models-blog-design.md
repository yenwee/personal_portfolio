# Blog Post Design: World Models (AMI Labs vs World Labs)

## Title
Two Scientists Bet a Billion Dollars Each That LLMs Are Not Enough. They Disagree on What Is.

## Subtitle
Yann LeCun thinks AI needs to understand physics. Fei-Fei Li thinks it needs to understand space. Only one of those problems has a market the size of the real world.

## Metadata
- Slug: `world-models-billion-dollar-bet`
- Tags: AI, Economics, Production
- Read time: ~8 minutes
- Featured: false
- Stance: LeCun's physics bet is stronger than Li's spatial bet

## Voice profile (preserved throughout)
- No contractions (do not, cannot, it is)
- Short declarative sentences for rhythm
- Open with specific vivid fact
- Personal authority without credential-dropping
- Convergence trick (multiple sources, same finding)
- Provocative section headers
- Close with callback + italicized one-liner

## Structure
1. **Hook** (~250w): Two billion-dollar seed rounds, same diagnosis, different prescriptions
2. **The coffee cup test** (~300w): World models explained through everyday physics (coffee spill, construction zone, nurse, glass bottle)
3. **The spatial intelligence bet** (~250w): What Fei-Fei Li is building, respect it, show the ceiling (eyes vs intuition framing)
4. **The physics bet** (~300w): What LeCun is building, JEPA through analogies (autocomplete vs imagination, cookbook vs baking), why he left Meta, healthcare first target
5. **Why one bet matters more** (~250w): Production AI insight -- perception solved, prediction not. Different examples from section 2 to avoid repetition
6. **What happens if LeCun is right** (~250w): LLMs as dial-up, the investor list as the tell, stakes if wrong

## Key research (from AMI Labs investigation)
- AMI: $1.03B seed, $3.5B pre-money, Yann LeCun + Alex LeBrun
- World Labs: $1B, Fei-Fei Li, Marble product shipping
- AMI investors: NVIDIA, Samsung, Temasek, Toyota Ventures, Bezos Expeditions
- AMI first target: healthcare via Nabla partnership
- JEPA: Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (non-generative, physics-first)
- LeCun left Meta over LLM scaling disagreement
- Gary Marcus criticism re: prior academic work attribution
